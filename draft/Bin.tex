\subsection{MTMH with non informative prior}
Apparently, the only way to solve the problem while being able to sample the whole $\Phi$ matrix in one step is to assume a sparse prior, which gives the same probability to all the matrices that are symmetric and positive definite, and to assume that the IW distribution is symmetric (which is not(?)). \\
Doing that, the algorithm is the same as the previous one, with the only difference that the weights become: 
\begin{equation}
    w(\Phi_j^*, \Phi_{t-1}) = L(f_1, ..., f_t|\Phi_j^*) 
\end{equation}


The model, conditional on the first \(p\) lags, is
\[
Y \mid A \sim \mathrm{MN}_{k,n}(A Z^\top, \, \sigma^2 \Sigma, \, I_n),
\]
where \(\Sigma = C \Phi C^\top\) is the covariance across function coefficients.

---

\subsubsection{Prior}

Assume a matrix-normal prior on \(A\):
\[
A \sim \mathrm{MN}_{k,pk}(A_0, \, \sigma^2 \Sigma, \, V_0).
\]
The density is
\[
p(A) \propto
\exp\!\left\{
-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}
\mathrm{tr}\!\big(
\Sigma^{-1} (A - A_0) V_0^{-1} (A - A_0)^\top
\big)
\right\}.
\]

---

\subsubsection{Likelihood}

The likelihood is
\[
p(Y \mid A) \propto
\exp\!\left\{
-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}
\mathrm{tr}\!\big(
\Sigma^{-1} (Y - A Z^\top)(Y - A Z^\top)^\top
\big)
\right\}.
\]

Expanding the trace:
\[
(Y - A Z^\top)(Y - A Z^\top)^\top
= YY^\top - YZ A^\top - A Z^\top Y^\top + A Z^\top Z A^\top.
\]
Therefore,
\[
-2\sigma^2 \log p(Y \mid A)
= \mathrm{tr}(\Sigma^{-1} A Z^\top Z A^\top)
- 2\, \mathrm{tr}(\Sigma^{-1} A Z^\top Y^\top) + \text{const.}
\]

---

\subsubsection{Combine prior and likelihood}

From the prior we get
\[
-2\sigma^2 \log p(A)
= \mathrm{tr}(\Sigma^{-1} A V_0^{-1} A^\top)
- 2\, \mathrm{tr}(\Sigma^{-1} A V_0^{-1} A_0^\top) + \text{const.}
\]

Summing both terms gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
-2\sigma^2 \log p(A \mid Y)
&= \mathrm{tr}\!\big( \Sigma^{-1} A (Z^\top Z + V_0^{-1}) A^\top \big) \\
&\quad - 2\, \mathrm{tr}\!\big( \Sigma^{-1} A (Z^\top Y^\top + V_0^{-1} A_0^\top) \big)
+ \text{const.}
\end{aligned}
\]

Define the sufficient statistics:
\[
S_{zz} = Z^\top Z, \qquad S_{yz} = Y Z.
\]

Then,
\[
V_n^{-1} = V_0^{-1} + S_{zz}, \qquad
S = S_{yz} + A_0 V_0^{-1}.
\]

Hence,
\[
-2\sigma^2 \log p(A \mid Y)
= \mathrm{tr}\!\big(\Sigma^{-1} A V_n^{-1} A^\top\big)
- 2\, \mathrm{tr}\!\big(\Sigma^{-1} A S^\top\big) + \text{const.}
\]

---

\subsubsection{Completing the square}

We use the identity
\[
\mathrm{tr}\!\big(\Sigma^{-1}(A - A_n)V_n^{-1}(A - A_n)^\top\big)
= \mathrm{tr}\!\big(\Sigma^{-1}A V_n^{-1}A^\top\big)
- 2\,\mathrm{tr}\!\big(\Sigma^{-1}A A_n V_n^{-1}\big)
+ \text{const.}
\]
Choosing \(A_n\) such that \(A_n V_n^{-1} = S^\top\), i.e.
\[
\boxed{
A_n = S V_n = (Y Z + A_0 V_0^{-1}) V_n,
}
\]
we obtain
\[
-2\sigma^2 \log p(A \mid Y)
= \mathrm{tr}\!\big(\Sigma^{-1}(A - A_n)V_n^{-1}(A - A_n)^\top\big) + \text{const.}
\]

Therefore, the posterior is again matrix-normal:
\[
\boxed{
A \mid Y, \sigma^2, \Sigma
\sim
\mathrm{MN}_{k,pk}(A_n, \, \sigma^2 \Sigma, \, V_n),
}
\]
with
\[
\boxed{
V_n = (V_0^{-1} + Z^\top Z)^{-1}, \qquad
A_n = (Y Z + A_0 V_0^{-1}) V_n.
}
\]

---

\subsubsection{Extracting the individual blocks}

Writing \(A = [A_1 \mid \cdots \mid A_p]\), the marginal of each \(A_r\) is matrix-normal, and the correlations among blocks are encoded in \(V_n\).  
If \(V_0\) is block-diagonal, \(V_n\) will in general not remain block-diagonal because of the cross-lag correlations in \(Z^\top Z\).

Conditionally, each \(A_r \mid A_{-r}, Y\) is also matrix-normal, with the same form applied to the residuals
\[
Y_r = Y - \sum_{s \neq r} A_s Z_s^\top.
\]

---

\subsubsection{Dimensions (for reference)}

\[
\begin{array}{ll}
Y: & k \times n, \\
Z: & n \times pk, \\
A: & k \times pk, \\
V_0, V_n: & pk \times pk, \\
\Sigma: & k \times k, \\
S_{yz}: & k \times pk, \\
S_{zz}: & pk \times pk.
\end{array}
\]

\begin{itemize}
    \item The response $y_t = f_t - \mu_t$ projected in the same space of the $p=1$ case, with row covariance $\Sigma = C \Phi C^T$;
    \item lagged regressors $z_t = [ \boldsymbol{\gamma}_{t-1}^T, ..., \gamma_{t-p}^T] \in \mathbb{R}^{1\times pk}$;
    \item concatenated parameters matrix: 
    \begin{equation}
        A = [A_1, A_2, ..., A_p] \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times pk}
    \end{equation}
\end{itemize}

We stack over time and we get
\begin{equation}
    Y = [y_{p+1}, ..., y_T] \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times (T-p)}, \qquad Z =  \left[\begin{matrix}
        z_{p+1} \\ \vdots \\ z_T
    \end{matrix} \right] \in \mathbb{R}^{(T-p)\times pk} 
\end{equation}
The model becomes
\begin{equation}
    Y = AZ^T + E, \qquad E \sim MN_{k, T-p}(0, \sigma^2 \Sigma, I_{T-p})
\end{equation}

We can out the same prior ad earlier, with
\begin{equation}
    A \sim MN_{k, pk}(A_0, V_0, \sigma^2 \Sigma)
\end{equation}
